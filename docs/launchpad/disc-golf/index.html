Disc Golf

Disc Golf Getting Started Guide

Disc golf is a fun and accessible outdoor sport that combines elements of golf and frisbee. Whether you’re a beginner looking for a new recreational activity or aiming to join the community, this guide will help you get started on the course.

Average Starting Cost:

Average Starting Cost: The initial investment is relatively affordable, with an average starting cost ranging from $20 to $50. This includes the purchase of a few discs, which are the primary equipment needed to play. However, there are budget-friendly alternatives for those looking to start with minimal spending.

Cheaper Alternatives:

  1. Buy Used Discs: Look for used discs at local shops or from other players. Used discs are often more affordable and can be an excellent way to build your collection.
  2. Starter Sets: Consider purchasing a beginner’s set, which typically includes multiple discs suited for beginners at a lower overall cost than buying individual discs.

Average Time Commitment:

The time commitment is flexible, making it suitable for casual players and enthusiasts alike. A round of typically takes 1 to 2 hours, depending on the course’s length and your pace. Players can enjoy a quick game or spend more time honing their skills during longer sessions.

First Steps to Take:

  1. Learn the Basics:
    • Rules: Familiarize yourself with the basic rules of disc golf, including how to score, the order of play, and common etiquette on the course.
    • Scoring: Disc golf typically follows the rules of traditional golf, where players aim to complete the course in the fewest throws (strokes) possible.
  2. Get the Right Discs:
    • Driver, Midrange, Putter: Invest in a basic set of discs, including a driver for long shots, a midrange for approach shots, and a putter for short-range shots.
    • Weights and Plastics: Consider different disc weights and plastic types to find discs that suit your throwing style. Lighter discs are generally easier for beginners.
  3. Practice Your Throws:
    • Backhand and Forehand Throws: Practice both backhand and forehand (also known as sidearm) throwing techniques to develop a well-rounded game.
    • Putting Practice: Spend time practicing putting to improve your accuracy and consistency on the greens.
  4. Explore Local Courses:
    • Find Nearby Courses: Use online resources or disc golf apps to locate nearby courses. Many parks and recreational areas have free or low-cost disc golf courses.
    • Join Local Groups: Connect with local disc golf communities or groups. They often host events, leagues, and can provide valuable tips for beginners.

Additional Resources:

  1. Websites and Apps: Explore websites like PDGA (Professional Disc Golf Association) and use apps like UDisc for course maps, scorekeeping, and connecting with the community.
  2. Tutorials and Videos: Watch tutorials on throwing techniques, course strategies, and game tips. Platforms like YouTube feature a wealth of instructional content from experienced disc golfers.
  3. Magazines and Books: Explore magazines or instructional books like “The Definitive Guide to Disc Golf” by Justin Menickelli for in-depth insights and advice.

Communities:

  1. Reddit – r/discgolf: A vibrant community where disc golfers of all levels share experiences, ask questions, and discuss the sport.
  2. Disc Golf Course Review: A comprehensive site with course reviews, forums, and a community of others sharing insights about various courses.
  3. Disc Golf Scene: Connect with locals, find events, and track your scores. A great platform for those looking to get involved in the community.
  4. Disc Golf United (DGU): Join tournaments, find courses, and connect with others through this platform that promotes community engagement.
  5. Ultiworld Disc Golf: A news and media platform with forums, articles, and resources for players of all levels.
  6. Infinite Discs Community: In addition to being an online retailer, Infinite Discs has a community section where disc golfers can discuss gear, courses, and more.
  7. Disc Golf Reddit Beginner’s FAQ: A helpful resource for beginners on the r/discgolf subreddit, offering frequently asked questions and valuable information.
  8. Dynamic Discs Community: Engage with the community through forums and discussions on this site associated with Dynamic Discs, a popular brand.
  9. Disc Golf Course Locator: The PDGA (Professional Disc Golf Association) course directory is a great resource for finding courses worldwide.
  10. Facebook Groups: Search for local groups on Facebook, where you can connect with players in your area, get advice, and stay updated on local events.

FAQs:

Q: How many discs do I need to start? A: A beginner can start with a basic set of three discs – a driver, a midrange, and a putter. As you progress, you may choose to expand your collection based on your playing style.

Q: Can I play alone? A: Absolutely! It is suitable for solo play, and many players enjoy the solitary aspect of the game. However, it’s also a social sport, and playing with friends or joining local leagues can enhance the experience.

Q: Do I need special shoes or equipment? A: While specialized shoes exist, they are not necessary for beginners. Comfortable athletic shoes with good grip are sufficient. Apart from discs, all you need is a positive attitude and a willingness to learn.

Disc golf is a sport that welcomes players of all skill levels. Enjoy the outdoors, embrace the challenge, and have fun as you navigate the disc golf course and develop your skills over time.
